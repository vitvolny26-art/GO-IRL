# GO IRL Bible

# Book I — Foundation

## Chapter 2

# Core Principles

Version 1.0

---

# Introduction

Every product eventually becomes a reflection of the decisions made by its creators.

Technology changes.

Programming languages change.

Frameworks disappear.

But principles remain.

These principles are the foundation of GO IRL.

Every product decision, every line of code, every design choice and every new feature must respect them.

If a decision violates these principles, the decision is wrong.

---

# Principle 1

## Real Life First

Real life is always more important than screen time.

GO IRL exists to help people leave the application.

Not stay inside it.

---

# Principle 2

## Human First

People are more important than algorithms.

Recommendations should help users.

Never manipulate them.

---

# Principle 3

## Simplicity Wins

Every important action should require as few steps as possible.

Creating an activity should take less than 30 seconds.

Joining an activity should take less than 10 seconds.

---

# Principle 4

## Offline Success

Success is measured outside the application.

Not inside it.

Every confirmed offline activity is more valuable than another hour of screen time.

---

# Principle 5

## Platform First

GO IRL is a platform.

Telegram is only the first client.

Every architectural decision must support future clients:

Telegram

Web

Android

iPhone

WhatsApp

Future integrations.

---

# Principle 6

## API First

Business logic belongs only to Backend.

Frontend only displays information.

Every client uses the same API.

---

# Principle 7

## Backend First

Business rules must never be duplicated in clients.

The Backend is the single source of truth.

---

# Principle 8

## Database First

Every feature starts with data modelling.

Before writing UI developers must understand:

What data exists?

Where is it stored?

Who owns it?

How does it evolve?

---

# Principle 9

## Modular Architecture

GO IRL is not one application.

It is a collection of independent modules.

Every module has:

its own UX,

its own filters,

its own event cards,

its own creation flow,

its own recommendation engine.

Shared services remain common.

---

# Principle 10

## Shared Core

Authentication.

Users.

Activities.

Notifications.

Chat.

Calendar.

RLI.

Trust Score.

Community Score.

Achievements.

These services belong to the Platform Core.

Modules must never duplicate them.

---

# Principle 11

## Privacy by Design

Collect the minimum amount of personal data.

If data is not required—

do not collect it.

If data is no longer required—

delete it.

Privacy is a feature.

---

# Principle 12

## Trust Must Be Earned

Reputation cannot be bought.

Reputation cannot be farmed.

Trust is earned only through verified real-life participation.

---

# Principle 13

## Quality over Quantity

One meaningful activity is more valuable than one hundred empty events.

The platform should encourage quality communities.

Not noise.

---

# Principle 14

## Community over Virality

Growth is important.

Healthy communities are more important.

Never sacrifice trust for rapid growth.

---

# Principle 15

## Accessibility

Everyone should understand GO IRL within the first minute.

The interface should never require instructions.

---

# Principle 16

## International by Design

Cities.

Languages.

Currencies.

Time zones.

Identity providers.

Everything must support international expansion from day one.

Nothing should be hardcoded.

---

# Principle 17

## Automation without Losing Humanity

Automation should remove routine.

Never replace human relationships.

n8n and AI exist to save time.

Not to replace people.

---

# Principle 18

## Honest Gamification

RLI exists to encourage real participation.

Not addiction.

Points have meaning only when connected to real experiences.

---

# Principle 19

## Long-Term Thinking

Every architectural decision should still make sense in ten years.

Quick hacks become expensive later.

Choose sustainable solutions.

---

# Principle 20

## Build Once. Reuse Everywhere.

Every new feature must immediately become available for every supported client.

Business logic is written once.

UI adapts per platform.

---

# Principle 21

## Every Feature Needs a Reason

Before implementation every feature must answer four questions.

1.

What problem does it solve?

2.

Does it increase real-life interaction?

3.

Can it be implemented simpler?

4.

Will users still need it in five years?

If any answer is "No", reconsider the feature.

---

# Principle 22

## Technology Should Disappear

The best GO IRL experience happens after the user closes the application.

The application should become invisible the moment people meet.

That is the final goal of every feature we build.

---

# Engineering Oath

Every developer contributing to GO IRL agrees to build technology that encourages people to spend more time together, not more time online.

Every line of code should move users one step closer to real life.

Never further away.
