# ADR-0000: Why GO IRL Exists

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Status:** Foundational (Never Changes)
**Authors:** GO IRL Founders

## Mission

GO IRL exists to solve a real human problem:

**The screen is stealing our lives.**

We spend hours scrolling through apps looking for things to do, people to meet, places to go. But we rarely actually *do* anything.

GO IRL reverses this:

**Less scrolling. More living.**

## Core Belief

Real-life experiences are the most valuable thing.

Technology should minimize friction between intention and action.

## What GO IRL Does

It helps people:

1. **Discover** real-life activities
2. **Connect** with others
3. **Organize** together
4. **Build** trust
5. **Create** communities

## What GO IRL Does NOT Do

- Replace apps
- Compete with social networks
- Try to become everything

GO IRL is **focused**: help people organize real-life activities, nothing else.

## Never Compromise On

1. **Speed** — Intent to action <1 minute
2. **Trust** — Real identity, real verification
3. **Community** — Local, peer-to-peer
4. **Privacy** — User data is sacred

## Architecture Consequence

Every technical decision must serve this mission.

If a technology makes organizing activities harder, it goes.

If a feature pushes users to spend more time in the app, it's rejected.

## Success Looks Like

In 5 years: Millions of real-life activities organized through GO IRL. Strong local communities. People saying: "I met my best friend through GO IRL."
